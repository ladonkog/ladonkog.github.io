Model 18 Beechcraft Stability Analysis

Project Overview

Background

In my aircraft stability and controls class, I served as the assistant project leader for a semester-long project focused on a comprehensive stability analysis of the Beechcraft Model 18. This project required us to conduct an in-depth stability assessment, covering longitudinal, directional, and lateral stability. Through detailed hand calculations, we derived essential stability coefficients, further enhanced by meticulous research to obtain all relevant technical data for a reliable stability analysis.

In my role, I collaborated with the project leader to guide our team, ensuring that each member completed their calculations independently while promoting a supportive and collaborative environment. At the end of the semester, we consolidated our findings into a final report that detailed our methodologies and findings, maintaining consistency across individual results within a small margin of error. Our report also allowed us to analyze our approach, compare outcomes, and provide justifications for any discrepancies, demonstrating a thorough understanding of stability concepts.

Objectives

  • Research and acquire comprehensive data required to analyze the stability of the Beechcraft Model 18.
  • Perform detailed stability calculations to derive all stability coefficients for longitudinal, directional, and lateral stability.
  • Calculate an optimal center of gravity position to achieve approximately 10% longitudinal stability.
  • Develop an Excel model to iterate center of gravity values, ensuring 10% longitudinal stability and validating hand calculations.
  • Document methodologies, results, and analyses in a comprehensive final report to showcase team understanding and analytical rigor.

This project gave me valuable experience aligning academic and industry approaches to stability analysis, reinforcing skills in both individual calculation work and collaborative analysis.
