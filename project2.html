Aircraft Preliminary Design: Hybrid-Electric Military Logistics Aircraft

Project Overview

This project involved a collaboration with Verdego Aero, a leader in hybrid-electric power systems for aircraft. Our team was tasked with designing a hybrid-electric military logistics aircraft capable of meeting specific operational and performance criteria set forth by Verdego Aero. These requirements included:

  • Accommodating a crew of two.
  • Short Takeoff and Landing (STOL) capability in under 500 feet.
  • A payload capacity of 2,000 lbs (excluding crew).
  • A cruise speed of 175 knots.
  • Battery-only flight for certain phases, including a 20 nm cruise, ascent, descent, and short takeoff/landing.
  • A total range of 650 nautical miles.
  • Use of at least one Verdego Aero generator to support hybrid-electric propulsion.

In addition to these design goals, the team established several enhancements to further improve the aircraftâ€™s capabilities, such as:

  • The integration of Distributed Electric Propulsion (DEP) for improved efficiency.
  • Enhanced maneuverability compared to traditional cargo and utility aircraft.
  • The use of batteries for all takeoffs, ascents, descents, and landings.

Our team focused on optimizing the design for both aerodynamics and propulsion, ensuring that the aircraft met all of the operational goals while adhering to sustainable aviation practices.

Key Contributions

  • Performed initial tail sizing and geometry, optimizing aerodynamic efficiency and stability.
  • Selected hybrid-electric propulsion components and optimized battery packs to meet energy requirements.
  • Conducted stick-fixed stability analysis to evaluate aircraft control and performance.
